# VISUAL_IDENTITY.md

## Purpose

This file defines V1 visual identity rules for Sahib AI quick rounds.

## World direction

Sahib AI uses a surreal alien world with pink/purple environmental identity.

The world should feel:
- surreal
- premium
- futuristic
- slightly alien
- distinct from generic medieval/fantasy RTS

## Buildings

Buildable kingdom/base structures should feel realistic and regional.

Direction:
- Gulf/Saudi/Dubai/KAFD-inspired futuristic buildings.
- Premium modern towers.
- Recognizable regional architectural influence.
- Not generic medieval fantasy.

## Enemy readability

Enemies must visually contrast with the world.

Because the world is pink/purple, enemies should not be fully pink/purple in a way that blends into the background.

## Alien visual rules

Aliens should:
- be organic and intimidating
- use darker contrasting tones
- avoid crystals in the body
- use natural details such as skin texture, hair-like forms, ridges, tendons, or bio-organic surfaces
- be readable during combat
- look distinct from the pink/purple world

Do not make aliens look too close to copyrighted Avatar Na'vi designs. Inspiration is acceptable at a broad creature-design level, but final design should be original.

## Robot/exosuit visual rules

Robots/exosuits should:
- use black/gunmetal/dark metallic colors
- use red/purple warning accents
- feel like rogue AI / dangerous technology
- contrast clearly with alien creatures

## Hunter Exosuit Trooper visual

- Human-piloted powered combat suit/exosuit.
- Not a pure robot.
- Shows pilot/cockpit/visor hint.
- Dark armor with red/purple glow accents.
- V1 gameplay role after swap: Core/tower pressure.

## Breaker Bot visual

- Fully robotic AI.
- No human pilot.
- Bulky wall-breaker silhouette.
- Dark metal.
- Red warning lights.
- Heavy breaching/tooling elements.

## Hero visual

Hero is a futuristic Gulf/Saudi tactical commander.

Should feel:
- premium
- tactical
- regional
- modern/futuristic
- not fantasy wizard
- not generic sci-fi laser soldier

## Doors and intersections

Enemy spawns should use mechanical doors, not magical portals.

Door style:
- heavy sci-fi sliding/blast doors
- attached to perimeter walls
- maze/horror-game tension
- random opening sequence
- visible but unpredictable

## Blockout-first visual rule

Use placeholder/blockout assets before final polished 3D.

Gameplay systems must be tested before committing to final art.

## Do not do

Avoid:
- generic medieval towers
- enemies blending into pink world
- alien body crystals
- direct copyrighted likenesses
- over-polished assets before gameplay works
- visual clutter that hides combat readability
